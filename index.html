What is Diwali?


Diwali is the festival of lights! It is celebrated to ward off the evil. There are many others reasons people celebrate Diwali, though. People in different places celebrate in different ways. My family celebrates Diwali like many others in Andhra Pradesh, India where my parents grew up.


Why do we celebrate Diwali?


As I said before, there are many reasons to clebrate Diwali, but the main reason is to scare away the evil and bring in light. Diwlai is a really fun festival!


How does everyone celebrate Diwali?


The one thing that everyone does for Diwali is burn firecrackers and/or sparklers! They do it when it gets dark, since dark is believed to be the domain of the evil. Many people also light diyas, which are small clay lamps that are filled with oil and and a small thread, they light this thread with a matchstick. They decorate these diyas and put them around and/or outside their house. They also do a pooja before eating in the morning to the goddess, Laxmi(Hindhu goddess of wealth) to give wealth and prosterity. A pooja is a prayer ritual performed before you break your fast by Hindus to offer devotional worship to one or more dieties, to host and honor a guest, or to spiritually celebrate an event. Some people celebrate Diwali for more than one day. They do a pooja every day in the week of Diwali. Diwali is an important festival for many people.


How does my family celebrate Diwali?


My family does firecrackers and sparklers like evryone else, too. We do a pooja and offer sweets and food to the gods But, we do onion bombs, flower pots, sparklers, and firecrackers that spin on the floor! My mom makes special homemade foods such as pulihora(lemon rice) and buys tasty sweets such as kaju katli(marzipan cut into diamond shapes). We usually invite or go to a friend's house to celebrate. My favorite part of Diwali though, is spending time with my family. Every year, we look at photos from the previous year's Diwali, and take photos that we would look at next year. Celebrating Diwali is super fun!
